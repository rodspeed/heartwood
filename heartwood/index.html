Privacy Policy

Data isolation

Your notes, connections, and graph data are isolated to your account using Row-Level Security (RLS) at the database layer. Every query is scoped to your authenticated session. No other Heartwood user can access your content.

Administrative access

Heartwood runs on Supabase (hosted Postgres). Like any hosted database, infrastructure credentials exist that could theoretically bypass RLS. We use these credentials solely for database migrations and critical debugging — never to browse user content. We are working toward column-level encryption to eliminate even this possibility.

Encryption

Data is encrypted at rest via AES-256, provided by our infrastructure layer (AWS). Data in transit is encrypted via TLS. We do not currently offer end-to-end encryption, but it is on our roadmap.

AI processing

AI features (embeddings, reasoning, ontology) send note content to third-party APIs (OpenAI, Anthropic) for processing. These providers process data transiently under their respective data processing agreements. We do not train models on your content, and we select providers whose policies prohibit this as well.

Data deletion

You can delete individual notes at any time. If you delete your account, all associated data is permanently removed via cascading deletes at the database level.

Regenerate Ontology?

This will re-analyze your notes and may change node types, colors, and shapes. Your spatial memory of the graph may shift.
